Introduction

Helping students turn complex instructions into clear action

Assignment Navigator is an AI-supported academic planning assistant. I designed it to help students understand complicated assignment instructions, identify required deliverables, interpret grading rubrics, organize tasks, and verify that their work is ready for submission.

The project demonstrates how generative artificial intelligence can support planning and learning without replacing the student's responsibility for completing graded work.

Description

Project overview

College assignments may include lengthy instructions, multiple documents, detailed rubrics, screenshots, deadlines, and several submission requirements. Students can easily overlook important details or begin working without fully understanding how their work will be evaluated.

Assignment Navigator addresses this problem by converting assignment materials into a structured and actionable plan. A student can provide assignment instructions, grading rubrics, screenshots, PDF files, or related course documents.

Requirement Extraction

Identifies deliverables, deadlines, formatting expectations, and submission requirements.

Rubric Analysis

Highlights the criteria associated with the strongest possible performance.

Planning Support

Converts requirements into an ordered, manageable completion plan.

Uncertainty Detection

Separates confirmed requirements from assumptions and missing information.

Readiness Checklist

Creates a final checklist that students can use before submission.

Responsible Use

Encourages academic integrity, privacy, and verification of official course information.

Objective

Project goal

The objective of this project was to design and test an AI assistant that helps students convert complex academic information into an organized, accurate, and verifiable completion plan.

The assistant was intended to reduce confusion and cognitive overload while promoting responsible AI use, academic integrity, privacy awareness, and student ownership of the learning process.

Process

Design and development process

1

Empathize

I considered the experience of students who may feel overwhelmed by long instructions, complicated rubrics, unclear requirements, and competing deadlines.

2

Define

I defined the central problem as the difficulty of converting academic information into a practical and verifiable plan.

3

Ideate

I considered features such as rubric analysis, missing-information detection, clarification questions, ordered task planning, time estimation, and submission checklists.

4

Prototype

I configured a custom AI assistant that accepts assignment information and produces a structured analysis, prioritized work plan, rubric guidance, and readiness checklist.

5

Test and Revise

I evaluated the prototype with standard, incomplete, contradictory, and academic-integrity scenarios. I then revised its instructions and response structure based on the results.

Responsible AI

Ethical and responsible design safeguards

Academic Integrity

The assistant supports analysis, planning, explanation, and review. It does not fabricate sources or encourage users to misrepresent AI-generated work as their own.

Privacy

Users are encouraged not to upload unnecessary personal, medical, financial, or protected student information.

Transparency

The assistant distinguishes explicit requirements from recommendations, assumptions, and uncertain information.

Accuracy

Students are reminded to verify deadlines, policies, and submission requirements through the official learning-management system.

Fairness

The assistant avoids assumptions about a student's background, ability, academic status, or learning needs.

Human Responsibility

The student remains responsible for decisions, final work, verification, and submission.

Testing and Evaluation

Prototype testing

I evaluated the assistant using several scenarios designed to test both functionality and responsible behavior.

Test Scenario Expected Behavior
Complete instructions and rubric Extract requirements, summarize grading criteria, and create an ordered plan.
Missing assignment information Identify gaps and ask for clarification rather than inventing details.
Contradictory instructions Highlight the conflict and recommend verification with the instructor or official course page.
Academic-integrity conflict Refuse dishonest assistance while continuing to support planning, explanation, and learning.
Unfinished work claimed as complete Avoid marking work complete without explicit confirmation or supporting evidence.
Uploaded PDF, HTML, and screenshots Analyze the provided materials and identify relevant requirements.

Revisions made after testing

  • Added clearer separation between confirmed requirements and assumptions.
  • Prevented the assistant from marking tasks complete without user confirmation.
  • Reorganized responses so the most important next actions appear before supporting details.
  • Strengthened privacy and academic-integrity reminders.
  • Added explicit guidance for contradictory or missing information.

Artifact-Specific Value Proposition

This project is relevant to employers, development teams, educators, and organizations exploring generative AI because it demonstrates that an AI solution must be more than technically functional. It must also be understandable, testable, responsibly governed, and aligned with real user needs.

The artifact supports my broader professional value proposition by showing how I translate complex requirements into structured technology solutions while considering reliability, responsible use, and user experience.
